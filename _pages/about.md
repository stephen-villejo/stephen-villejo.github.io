I am currently a Research Associate in Biostatistics at the Department of Epidemiology and Biostatistics, Faculty of Medicine, at Imperial College London. I am also a member of the Environment and Health Statistics [Group](https://sites.google.com/view/envstats/home?authuser=0) under the Medical Research Council Centre for Environment & [Health](https://environment-health.ac.uk/). 

Currently, I am working on a MRC-funded project on incorporating wastewater-based epidemiology into a real-time, multiplex public health surveillance system under the supervision of Professor Marta [Blangiardo](https://profiles.imperial.ac.uk/m.blangiardo).

Before joining Imperial, I completed my PhD in Statistics at the University of Glasgow, supervised by Professor Janine [Illian](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=H31cYt8AAAAJ&hl=en). Prior to my doctoral studies, I was a faculty member at the School of Statistics at the University of the Philippines [Diliman](https://upd.edu.ph/), where I also earned my MS in Statistics under the supervision of Professor Erniel [Barrios](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=pQkAfg8AAAAJ&hl=en).  


Research Interests
======

My research interests include developing methods for spatially and temporally misaligned data, with applications in epidemiology and public health. Specific topics of interests are block aggregation models for point-to-areal data, data fusion methods, uncertainty propagation in multi-stage models, and mixed data sampling models for multi-frequency time series problems.
